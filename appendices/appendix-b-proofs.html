Mục đích

Phụ lục B chứa các chứng minh toán học cho các công thức quan trọng xuất hiện trong các chương chính. Trong các chương, công thức được phát biểu và sử dụng; chứng minh chi tiết được chuyển về Phụ lục B để giữ mạch đọc chính không bị gián đoạn. Người đọc cần thực sự hiểu sâu (viết paper, giải thích cho team) nên tham khảo phụ lục này; người đọc thực hành có thể bỏ qua.

Các chứng minh hiện có: DFT completeness, CTC forward-backward derivation, tính chất triangular của mel filterbank.

Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT) Completeness

Statement

DFT basis vectors form an orthogonal set. Any discrete signal of length N can be perfectly reconstructed from its N DFT coefficients.

Proof

Orthogonality of DFT basis vectors:

Define DFT basis vector wk​=[1,e−j2πk/N,e−j2πk⋅2/N,…,e−j2πk(N−1)/N].

⟨wk​,wl​⟩=n=0∑N−1​e−j2πkn/N⋅ej2πln/N=n=0∑N−1​ej2π(l−k)n/N

Case 1: k=l

n=0∑N−1​ej2π⋅0⋅n/N=n=0∑N−1​1=N

Case 2: k=l, let r=l−k=0

n=0∑N−1​ej2πrn/N=1−ej2πr/N1−ej2πr​=1−ej2πr/N1−1​=0

since ej2πr=1 for integer r.

Therefore: ⟨wk​,wl​⟩=N⋅δkl​ - orthogonal basis. ■

CTC Forward-Backward Algorithm

Statement

The CTC loss can be efficiently computed in O(T×U) time using dynamic programming, where T is the number of frames and U is the label length.

Forward Variable

Define αt​(s) = probability of outputting the first s symbols of the modified label sequence ℓ′ (with blanks) in t time steps:

αt​(s)=P(π1:t​ maps to ℓ1:s′​∣x)

Initialization (t=1):

α1​(1)=P(blank∣x1​),α1​(2)=P(ℓ1​∣x1​),α1​(s)=0 for s>2

Recursion (t>1):

αt​(s)=P(ℓs′​∣xt​)⋅{αt−1​(s)+αt−1​(s−1)αt−1​(s)+αt−1​(s−1)+αt−1​(s−2)​if ℓs′​=blank or ℓs′​=ℓs−2′​otherwise​

Proof of correctness:

At time t, the token at position s in ℓ′ can be reached from:

  1. Same position s at t−1: Repeating the same token
  2. Previous position s−1 at t−1: Moving to next token
  3. Two positions back s−2 at t−1: Skipping a blank (only if ℓs′​=ℓs−2′​, i.e., no repeated characters)

Case 3 is excluded when ℓs′​=blank or when two consecutive non-blank tokens are the same, because skipping the blank between them would cause a merge. ■

Total CTC Loss

P(ℓ∣x)=αT​(∣ℓ′∣)+αT​(∣ℓ′∣−1)

The two terms account for paths ending with the last label or with a trailing blank.

LCTC​=−logP(ℓ∣x)

Conditional Flow Matching Derivation

Statement

The Conditional Flow Matching (CFM) objective provides a simulation-free training procedure for continuous normalizing flows.

Setup

We want to learn a time-dependent velocity field vθ​(x,t) that transforms a simple distribution p0​ (e.g., Gaussian) into a target distribution p1​ (e.g., data) via the ODE:

dtdxt​​=vθ​(xt​,t),t∈[0,1]

Optimal Transport Path

Choose the linear interpolation path (optimal transport):

xt​=(1−t)x0​+tx1​,x0​∼p0​,x1​∼p1​

The conditional velocity field generating this path is:

ut​(x∣x1​)=x1​−x0​

Proof: Taking the time derivative of the interpolation:

dtdxt​​=dtd​[(1−t)x0​+tx1​]=−x0​+x1​=x1​−x0​

Training Objective

The CFM loss matches the learned velocity to the conditional velocity:

LCFM​(θ)=Et∼U[0,1],x0​∼p0​,x1​∼p1​​[∥vθ​(xt​,t)−(x1​−x0​)∥2]

Key result 1: Minimizing this conditional objective is equivalent to minimizing the marginal flow matching objective:

LFM​(θ)=Et,xt​∼pt​​[∥vθ​(xt​,t)−ut​(xt​)∥2]

The proof relies on the fact that ∇θ​LCFM​=∇θ​LFM​ (gradients are equal), making CFM a valid training procedure. ■

Gumbel-Softmax Gradient Estimator

Statement

Gumbel-Softmax provides a differentiable approximation to sampling from a categorical distribution, enabling gradient-based optimization through discrete choices.

Gumbel-Max Trick

To sample from a categorical distribution with logits ℓ1​,…,ℓK​:

k∗=argkmax​[ℓk​+gk​],gk​∼Gumbel(0,1)

where gk​=−log(−log(uk​)),uk​∼Uniform(0,1).

Continuous Relaxation

Replace argmax with softmax at temperature τ:

yk​=∑j=1K​exp((ℓj​+gj​)/τ)exp((ℓk​+gk​)/τ)​

Properties:

  • As τ→0: y approaches one-hot (exact categorical sample)
  • As τ→∞: y approaches uniform distribution
  • For any τ>0: y is differentiable w.r.t. ℓk​

Proof of convergence:

τ→0lim​yk​={10​if k=argmaxj​(ℓj​+gj​)otherwise​

This holds because as τ→0, softmax concentrates all mass on the maximum element. ■

Application in Wav2Vec 2.0

Wav2Vec 2.0 uses Gumbel-Softmax to make codebook selection differentiable during training:

q=k=1∑K​yk​⋅ek​,yk​=GumbelSoftmax(ℓk​,τ)

Temperature τ is annealed from 2.0 to 0.5 during training.
